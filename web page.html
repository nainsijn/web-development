ABOUT ANIMALS

Wild Life

We were all told tales as children – many of which are considered superstition and relatively harmless. Growing up in India, we’ve often heard things like “don’t stray across the path of a black cat,” or “an itch on the palm means wealth is headed your way” and the like. Amongst many others, our country’s wildlife has faced a rising threat from superstitious beliefs over the years, and these are far from innocuous. From the belief that elephant tusks and elephant hair bring good luck to the faith that sacrificing owls in rituals can bring fortune – many such concepts continue to exist even today, endangering the lives of countless wild animals across the country. This can affect entire ecosystems, and despite a strong legal framework in place, these unfortunate practices continue under the radar...
